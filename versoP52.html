P52 Manuscript, Verso: John 18:37-38

Verso (Back Side)

The verso is the back side of the papyrus.
Writing on both sides reflects the early Christian use of the codex, conserving materials and making Scripture easier to carry and distribute.

This side contains Jesus’ declaration that He came to testify to the truth.

P52 Verso • John 18:37-38

Greek Text (Reconstructed)

… Σὺ λέγεις ὅτι βασιλεύς εἰμι.
Ἐγὼ εἰς τοῦτο γεγέννημαι
καὶ εἰς τοῦτο ἐλήλυθα εἰς τὸν κόσμον,
ἵνα μαρτυρήσω τῇ ἀληθείᾳ·
πᾶς ὁ ὢν ἐκ τῆς ἀληθείας
ἀκούει μου τῆς φωνῆς.

Λέγει αὐτῷ ὁ Πιλᾶτος·
Τί ἐστιν ἀλήθεια;

Greek Transliteration

… Sy legeis hoti basileus eimi.
Egō eis touto gegennēmai
kai eis touto elēlytha eis ton kosmon,
hina martyrēsō tē alētheia;
pas ho ōn ek tēs alētheias
akouei mou tēs phōnēs.

Legei autō ho Pilatos:
Ti estin alētheia?

English Translation (NIV)

“You are right in saying I am a king.
In fact, for this reason I was born, and for this I came into the world,
to testify to the truth.
Everyone on the side of truth listens to Me.”

“What is truth?” retorted Pilate.

Historical Context

The verso (back side) of P52 contains one of the most profound exchanges in Scripture.

Here:
• Jesus defines His mission
• Kingship is redefined as testimony
• Truth stands before political power

That early Christians chose to preserve this moment speaks volumes. These words were copied not because they were safe—but because they were true.


Reflection

Pilate asks,

“What is truth?”

Jesus has already answered.

This fragment reminds us that the truth was spoken plainly, written faithfully, and carried forward.

At Christmas, we remember,
The Word became flesh.
Born in humility. Written in faith. Preserved through time.
